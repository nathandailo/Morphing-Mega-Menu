How this navigation works

The nav stays fixed and floating above this content. Below is a breakdown of how each part behaves.

Six submenu styles

Each top-level parent opens one of six layouts:

  • Image grid: a grid of cards with thumbnails, titles and descriptions.
  • CTA + image grid: the same cards alongside a promotional CTA panel.
  • Icon grid: a grid of icon, title and description links.
  • CTA + icon grid: icon links alongside a CTA panel.
  • Compact icons: a narrow dropdown of icon and text rows.
  • Compact links: a narrow dropdown of plain text links.

One shell, sliding between parents

Every submenu renders inside the same panel "shell" rather than each parent owning its own dropdown. Hovering a different parent slides the current content out and the new content in from the matching side, while the shell itself resizes and repositions to fit the new layout, with mega grids on one side and narrow compact dropdowns on the other.

Drilling into a third level

Some items show an arrow indicating they open a third level. Clicking one morphs the shell into a compact dropdown anchored beneath that item's parent, with a back link at the top to return.

Because the dropdown can land away from the cursor, a third-level view stays open ("pinned") until you click the back link, click elsewhere on the page, or press Escape. It won't disappear just because the mouse drifts off the nav. Going back morphs the shell to a full mega layout, or keeps it compact if the parent was already a compact-style menu.

Mobile drill navigation

On smaller screens the menu becomes a sliding, full-width drill-down: parents slide to children, and children with their own third level slide further to subchildren. The nav bar's back button steps back one level at a time, and while viewing a third level it relabels itself with that item's parent name (truncated with an ellipsis if it's longer than four words).
